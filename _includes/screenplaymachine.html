Act 1

Part 1: Establish Status Quo + Inciting Action

  • Show how your protagonist feels starting emotion since he or she lacks protagonist internal goal.
  • Demonstrate your protagonist's skill of input. This positive quality qualifies him or her for the job and gains the audience's support.
  • Show the worlds need for external goal. This need can be embodied in a character or physical object.
  • Show how your antagonist inflicts external risk by eliminating external goal. This is your inciting action.

Part 2: Establish the Lock-in

  • Your protagonist reacts to the impact of external risk and/or the need for external goal.
  • Introduce your protagonists objective to re-establish or acquire external goal by preventing external risk.
  • Show how the objective is useful to your protagonist, because it promotes protagonist internal goal (while preventing protagonist internal risk).
  • Your protagonist locks into objective.

Act 2.1:

Part 3: Earning Education Trait

  • Demonstrate "coping mechanism." This is a coping mechanism that allows your protagonist to adjust to his or her objective. It, however, will reach a boiling point that bursts at the midpoint.
  • Your protagonist learns your antagonists goal for antagonist internal goal and risk of antagonist internal risk.
  • Introduce Build-ups & Payoffs. These are setting, tools, Chekhovs gun, any other characters necessary for your protagonist to progress his or her objective.
  • Once all necessary characters are introduced, your protagonist earns his or her "Education trait". He or she is now forced to act upon this understanding in what has changed the status quo.

Part 4: Escalation to Midpoint

  • Your protagonist encounters his or her first major obstacle related to external goal.
  • By either acting or being thrown into this obstacle, your protagonist uses his or her input and Education Trait to get closer to achieving external goal.
  • Regardless of whether he was successful in overcoming the obstacle, the progress must ultimately change the direction of the films journey by eliminating coping mechanism
  • Your protagonist loses his or her coping mechanism, which leaves him or her exposed to the potential risk of protagonist internal risk. He or she must achieve external goal the hard way.

Act 2.2.

Part 5: Consequences

  • Without coping mechanism, your protagonists protagonist internal risk becomes closer than his or her protagonist internal goal. Show how this happens.
  • With this risk, your protagonist questions the objective all together. He or she considers/attempts another approach while External Risk becomes closer.
  • While this objective may temporarily avoid protagonist internal risk, it does not achieve the protagonist internal goal that your protagonist has sought after throughout the film.
  • At the end of this sequence, your protagonist earns his or her Failure Trait. This may be the missing piece.

Act 3:

Part 6: The Climax

  • Your protagonist returns to trying to accomplish his or her objective by using his or her input, Education Trait, and Failure Trait.
  • In both the set-up and the climax, Build-ups & Payoffs are resolved as supporting characters and Chevhovs Guns allows your protagonist to get to achieve external goal
  • Your protagonist directly confronts your antagonist. This interaction determines that your protagonist achieves/fails to achieve external goal.

Part 7: The Resolution

  • Demonstrate the effects of achieving/failing to achieve external goal. Are the as bigger/smaller than expected?
  • Reveal whether all the characters achieve their internal needs.
  • Since your protagonist achieves/fails to achieve protagonist internal goal, he or she feels ending emotion.
